About the asset

What PBS 2D is

PBS 2D is a starting point for 2D games that rely on physics-driven characters rather than pre-baked animation. Instead of sprite sheets, the character is a chain of rigid bodies connected by joints, balanced by spring forces, and animated through inverse kinematics while conscious — and flipped into a full ragdoll the moment they're not.

On top of that lives a complete weapon system: interactable guns with realistic reload and cycling animations, per-frame raycast projectiles that support penetration, and support for self-cycling, bolt-action, and pump-action firearms. Input is routed through Unity's Input System and works out of the box with keyboard & mouse, gamepad, and touch.

It's intended for developers who want a solid, readable foundation to build 2D action, shooter, or physics-puzzle games on top of — not a plug-and-play demo.

Features

What's included

Active ragdoll

Characters switch seamlessly between IK-driven control and a physics ragdoll, with balance springs that soften the transition on hit, stun, or death.

Procedural locomotion

Feet are placed dynamically each step using raycast fans and torso-relative targets. Walk, run, and crouch each have their own tuning, and feet step over small obstacles automatically.

Modular weapon system

Guns inherit from a shared base and use ScriptableObject stats. Self-cycling (auto / semi / burst), bolt-action, and pump-action cycle types are supported out of the box.

Realistic reload & cycling

Coroutine-driven animations move the off hand through magazine and slide positions with per-gun timing, driven by the same IK rig used for locomotion.

Multi-device input

Built on Unity's Input System with keyboard & mouse, gamepad, and touch bindings. Aiming supports both world-point (pointer) and direction (stick) modes.

Data-driven tuning

Weapon stats, audio configs, and effect configs are ScriptableObjects, so balancing and sound design can happen without touching code.
